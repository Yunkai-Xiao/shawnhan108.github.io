If Not Now, When? If Not Us, Who?

View this post on Instagram

Today is #worldenvironmentday2019 . When living in an era in which societies are mainly powered by fossil fuels, oceans are stuffed with plastics, glaciers are fragmented into pieces, greenhouse gas level is at unprecedented high, and current extinction rate is 1000 times higher than natural background rates... there are countless reasons to give up. Yet, “it is during the darkest moments that we must focus on seeing the light”, that we must think and act beyond persons and families but as a species, and hope that evolution can transcend the simple barrier of individualism and selfishness. In this way, we can see the light; in this way, we can dream in a hopeful night. 2019 #worldenvironmentday theme is #beatairpollution . Choice of food, lifestyle, energy, transportation, and method of waste disposal... there are a myriad of ways that we can battle air pollution, or make it worse. If you’ve read The #thenewyorkers article The Day The Dinosaurs Died, you will know that there were always species that survived one of the five mass extinctions, and life on earth would still carry on. That is, saving the environment now is not saving this planet, but saving mankind itself. #worldenvironmentday #worldenvironmentday2019 #conservation #beatairpollution #artivism #lightroom #nopollution #fernplant #waterloo @natgeoyourshot @envwaterloo @unitednations

The water is so pristine, that to touch it is to defile it, to insult its being. I don't understand, but the wild, those landscapes and nature that are intact from human influence, always seems so attractive to me, and so in need for cherish. However, within mere hours, the Norwegian election will start. And Lofoten archipelago, somewhere equally stunning as(if not more beautiful than) this place, will be under the threat of oil drills. Nature is where I can feel the connection to this planet. Earth has no expectation on anything dwells on it but offers so much. It inspires us to live modestly, to see, to appreciate and be thankful. Today is #earthday2018 . This year’s theme is reducing plastic pollution. So please remember to recycle; to avoid using disposable plastics such as plastic straws; to avoid using plastic bottles. Together we can prove scientists wrong, that by 2050 there will be less plastic than fish in our ocean, that less than 75% sea turtles will ingest plastic, that less than one third of marine mammals will die because of microplastic-poisoned food source. #together we protect our planet.
